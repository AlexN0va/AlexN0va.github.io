About Me

Coming from a low-income environment and family, I have firsthand experience of helping my parents with everyday tasks, seeking their love, and dedicating myself to learning and education. Growing up, I was determined to achieve perfect grades as a way of showing gratitude to my parents for their sacrifices. After my first computer science class in high school, coupled with self-taught Python and Arduino projects, I realized that computer science and engineering were my gateways into a world of knowledge that I was determined to pursue. Now, after numerous classes, internships, and projects—such as a walking biped robot—computer science has become my magical resource for empowering my ideas.

Fluent in English and Spanish, I am committed to breaking barriers and uplifting my community, inspired by the values instilled in me by my Hispanic culture. I strive to navigate financial challenges by seeking scholarships, saving earnings, and maintaining a positive, resilient mindset. My goals extend beyond pursuing a career; I aim to leverage computer science to bridge gaps, inspire future generations, and create positive change both in my college environment and beyond. As I continue my journey in computer science and robotics, I aspire to engage in research that makes STEM education more effective and accessible.

P.S

Although I’d like to focus on using my skills for educational purposes, my diverse interests range from the automotive industry to energy and mental/physical health. I am eager to unite these interests while forging connections to address pressing societal challenges through computer science.

Work

Berkeley Bear Blogger is not just a blog about bears; it’s a platform designed to allow students to share their experiences and stories about their time at UC Berkeley. Through this fully implemented forum-based blogging application, students can connect, reflect, and engage with the Berkeley community by posting about their academic journeys, campus life, personal achievements, and more.

TITO is a fully 3D-printed robot that I designed to mimic the dance moves and walking patterns of humans. By studying multiple research papers on bipedal movement and motor control, I was able to develop and code TITO's dancing and walking features. The robot is controlled via an IR remote, allowing it to perform complex movements that closely resemble human behavior.

Segmented Image Compression is an AI-based MRI compression application designed to make it easy and convenient for doctors to transmit MRI images without losing crucial image quality. Leveraging Facebook's SAM model, this tool ensures that vital details are preserved during compression. This project was developed as part of the Stanford Compression Forum.
